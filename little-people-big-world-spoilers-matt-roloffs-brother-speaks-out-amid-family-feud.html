Matt Roloff's Brother Speaks Out Amid Family Feud

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Thursday, June 27, 2024

The Little People Big World spoilers reveal that Matt Roloff’s brother Sam spoke out amid the ongoing family feud. He feels that their situation is “sad” given the sale of Roloff Farm. Read on to see what else he had to say about his famous brother.

The Little People Big World Spoilers: Matt Roloff’s Brother Sam Speaks Out

According to the Little People, Big World spoilers, Sam spoke to The U.S. Sun about his upbringing with Matt Roloff. He resides in California, where he’s getting ready for his upcoming art exhibit in the de Young Museum.

Sam created digital art called The Rise of the Yellow Vests Act 23 – The Burning of Notre Dame, which will be on display at the San Franciso-based gallery until January 2024.

His second artwork, Discovery of a Giant Frog Skeleton, will also be on exhibit until next month. Sam made a brief appearance on the TLC series in the past. During his interview with The U.S. Sun, Sam admitted that both of them have always been creative.

“We both had that passion for creating worlds, if you will,” Sam told the outlet. “I remember even as a kid, Matt [and I] would have carnivals set up in the lot next door to our house. We were always doing something kind of wacky or building a fort.”

The Little People, Big World spoilers reveal their brotherly bond. Sam believes it had something to do with their dwarfism. Since they didn’t feel accepted by the outside world, they created their own world. They used their imaginations to be expressive and creative.

Little People Big World Spoilers: Sam Roloff Speaks On Roloff Farm

The Little People, Big World spoilers also reveal that Sam Roloff spoke out about the future of Roloff Farm. He finds it “sad” that the family farm went for sale in May 2022.

Matt sold 16 of the 109 acres to someone else. His sons Zach and Jeremy Roloff offered to purchase the land, but he rejected their offers. Instead, he put it up for sale.

Matt wanted $4 million for the family farm. This angered both his family members and fans of the show. Matt went against his family all because of money. His brother Sam finds it “sad” that someone could end up buying the farm and take everything away that was associated with it.

“It’s a good family farm and hopefully they keep it,” Sam said. “[Matt] spent so much time building it out – maybe he’s just tired of it, I don’t know. The cowboy town, the castle, all of those things – it’s sad to get rid of them all.”
