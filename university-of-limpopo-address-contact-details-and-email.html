University of Limpopo Address, Contact Details, and Email

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Wednesday, June 26, 2024

The University of Limpopo ranks among the top universities in South Africa. It was formed by the merger of the Unversity of the North, nicknamed ‘Turfloop,’ and the Medical University of South Africa (MEDUNSA) on January 1st, 2005. Previously, it had two campuses situated in Turfloop and Ga-Rankuwa, but now stays only in Turfloop.

For every category of inquiry, the management of the University of Limpopo has contact details for that. In this article, we’ve got you covered with complete UL contact details, including the physical and email addresses and the University of Limpopo admin contact details and finance contact.

University of Limpopo Address

The University of Limpopo Initially had two campuses; the Turfloop campus and the Medunsa campus. The former is erected on a site chosen by Dr. Hendrick Verwoerd, erstwhile Prime Minister in Turfloops, to the east of Polokwane in Limpopo province. It was at first launched as a tertiary college, but after a decade, it attained full university status.

The Turfloop campus is the manifestation of the university’s fundamental goal of becoming the leading university that addresses the needs of the African rural communities. The Medunsa campus, on the other hand, was incorporated into the University of Limpopo (UL) as a full medical faculty.

However, it got separated from UL in 2015. Medunsa is now part of a new university called Sefako Makgatho Health Sciences University (SMU). Despite the Turfloop campus being situated in the middle of a densely populated old homeland area, it boasts several centres of excellence. This includes the Materials Modelling Centre.

Riveting Facts About The University of Limpopo

  • The University of Limpopo is the first higher institution to have a medical school since the democratic era in 1994
  • UL’s Radio Turf was the first campus radio station to be licensed
  • UL launched the first comprehensive Department in Geology and Mining since the democratic dispensation
  • The University of Limpopo has produced a second and third generation of leaders in South Africa
  • President Cyril Ramaphosa and Finance Minister Tito Mboweni studied at the University of Limpopo
  • UL has a leading advanced disability centre that accommodates all types of persons living with disability
